OpenLvVision_OpenCv.lvlib:PyrDown.vim

Blurs an image and downsamples it.

By default, size of the output image is computed as Size((src.cols+1)/2, (src.rows+1)/2), but in any case, the following conditions should be satisfied:

{{< katex >}}$ \begin{aligned} | \texttt{dstsize.width} \cdot 2 - \texttt{src.cols} | &\leq 2 \\ | \texttt{dstsize.height} \cdot 2 - \texttt{src.rows} | &\leq 2 \end{aligned} ${{< /katex >}}

The function performs the downsampling step of the Gaussian pyramid construction. First, it convolves the source image with the kernel:

{{< katex >}}$ \frac{1}{256} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 4 & 6 & 4 & 1 \\ 4 & 16 & 24 & 16 & 4 \\ 6 & 24 & 36 & 24 & 6 \\ 4 & 16 & 24 & 16 & 4 \\ 1 & 4 & 6 & 4 & 1 \end{bmatrix} ${{< /katex >}}

Then, it downsamples the image by rejecting even rows and columns.

For detailed information, please refer to the OpenCV documentation

BorderTypes

Pixel extrapolation method, see BorderTypes (BORDER_CONSTANT isn't supported)

BORDER_CONSTANT = 0, !< `iiiiii|abcdefgh|iiiiiii` with some specified `i`

BORDER_REPLICATE = 1, `aaaaaa|abcdefgh|hhhhhhh`

BORDER_REFLECT = 2, `fedcba|abcdefgh|hgfedcb`

BORDER_WRAP = 3, `cdefgh|abcdefgh|abcdefg`

BORDER_REFLECT_101 = 4, `gfedcb|abcdefgh|gfedcba`

BORDER_TRANSPARENT = 5, `uvwxyz|abcdefgh|ijklmno` - Treats outliers as transparent.

BORDER_ISOLATED = 16 Interpolation restricted within the ROI boundaries.
